# Philosophy of AI: Metaphors, Epistemology, and Cognitive Limits

## Overview

**Status:** Migrated (TAM1 + TAM2)
**Created:** 2026-02-22
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-22

Exploratory articles on the philosophical and epistemological dimensions of AI: how AI functions as metaphor and mirror, and how classical frameworks (e.g., Plato's levels of cognition) illuminate current limitations and possibilities of language models.

## Key Questions

1. What are the deeper implications of AI beyond efficiency gains, and how should we approach systemic risks?
2. How do Plato's levels of cognition (Eikasia, Pistis, Dianoia, Noesis) help us understand where current AI systems stall?
3. How do we preserve epistemic diversity and human agency in an AI-mediated world?

## Central Concepts

- **Models as metaphors**: AI models as ways of ensnaring and making reality understandable
- **Plato's four levels**: Eikasia (illusion), Pistis (pattern recognition), Dianoia (discursive reasoning), Noesis (intellectual vision)
- **Stochastic parrot**: Critique of pattern matching vs genuine understanding
- **Spiritual intelligence**: Transcending existing models, focusing on origin of intuition, maintaining human agency
