name: Prudence over drama in factual claims
description: Author prefers to omit data or soften language rather than overstate — removal over hedging, verified over dramatic
type: feedback
---

When handling unverified, overstated, or misattributed claims, default to removing or softening rather than creative hedging.

**Why:** The author's credibility depends on precision. A smaller verified claim is always better than a larger unverified one. This was reinforced after a deep fact-check of Article 3 revealed fabricated Veracode multipliers that had been kept with a misleading parenthetical disclaimer — exactly the kind of false confidence the author wants to avoid.

**How to apply:** During fact-checking and editing, when a figure can't be confirmed in the cited source: (1) remove it entirely, or (2) replace with what the source actually says. Never invent disclaimers like "from the full report" to justify keeping an unverified figure. Apply this across all articles in the series.
