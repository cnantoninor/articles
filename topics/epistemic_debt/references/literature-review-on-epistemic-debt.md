1. Ionescu, T.B., Schlund, S., Schmidbauer, C. (2020). “Epistemic Debt: A Concept and Measure of Technical Ignorance in Smart Manufacturing” ￼ - This paper introduces epistemic debt as a tool for understanding technical ignorance in manufacturing systems.
∙ Link: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-20040-4_8
2. “Epistemic Opacity, Confirmation Holism and Technical Debt: Computer Simulation in the Light of Empirical Software Engineering” ￼ - Connects technical debt concepts to epistemic issues in computational science.
∙ Link: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-47286-7_18

## Epistemic Debt & LLM-Generated Code

### Primary paper:
“The Illusion of Competence: Defining ‘Epistemic Debt’ in the Era of LLM-Assisted Software Engineering” by Ludovic A. Ngabang (January 2, 2026) ￼
∙ Direct link: https://vixra.org/pdf/2601.0013v1.pdf
∙ Defines epistemic debt as “the divergence between the complexity of a software system and the developer’s cognitive model of that system” ￼
∙ Argues that in AI-assisted development, system complexity can rise exponentially while cognitive grasp remains flat ￼
∙ Introduces concepts like “Stochastic Spaghetti Effect” and “Context Window Amnesia”
∙ Proposes a “Cognitive Ratchet” methodology for mitigation
∙ It formally defines epistemic debt in the LLM context and provides mathematical framing for the concept.

### Complementary framework — Epistemia:
Loru, E., Nudo, J., Quattrociocchi, W. et al. (2025). "The Simulation of Judgment in Large Language Models." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)*.
∙ Link: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2518443122 (PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12557803/)
∙ Introduces *epistemia*: "the illusion of knowledge that emerges when surface plausibility replaces verification"
∙ LLMs rely on lexical associations and statistical patterns rather than contextual reasoning; they confuse linguistic form with epistemic reliability
∙ Complements epistemic debt: epistemia names the perceptual mechanism (we mistake plausibility for understanding); epistemic debt names the accumulated cost when that illusion scales across artifacts and teams
