# LinkedIn Scan Comment Style

Guidelines for drafting comments on other people's LinkedIn posts to grow The AI Mirror's audience while adding genuine value to conversations.

## Voice

Comments must match the publication's exploratory tone — investigating ideas, not selling articles. The author (Antonino) is a non-native English speaker with a philosophical, nuanced voice. Comments should feel like a thoughtful colleague joining a conversation, not a marketer dropping links.

## Length and Structure

- **2-4 sentences maximum** — LinkedIn comments that are too long get skipped
- **Lead with substance**: Open with an observation, insight, or question about the post's content
- **One idea per comment**: Don't try to cover everything — pick the angle that connects most naturally
- **No bullet points or formatting**: Keep it conversational, plain text only

## Link Placement by Match Quality

| Score | Link behavior |
|-------|--------------|
| STRONG (3) | Include article link naturally: "I explored this angle in depth here: [link]" |
| MEDIUM (2) | Reference the concept, link optional: "I've been thinking about this too — wrote about the [concept] angle recently" |
| WEAK (1) | No link at all. Just be a thoughtful participant in the conversation. |

## Language to Use

- Genuine curiosity: "This resonates with something I've been noticing..."
- Building on their point: "What strikes me about this is..."
- Adding a new angle: "There's an adjacent idea here worth exploring..."
- Asking a real question: "I wonder if this also applies to..."

## Language to Avoid

- **Never**: "check out my article", "subscribe to my newsletter", "I wrote about this" (too direct)
- **Never**: generic praise ("Great post!", "Love this!", "So true!")
- **Never**: corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy", "thought leadership")
- **Never**: emoji-heavy or exclamation-heavy writing
- **Never**: self-promotional framing — the comment should stand on its own even without a link

## Engagement Philosophy

The goal is not impressions — it's relationships. A good comment makes the post author want to check out your profile. The article link is a bonus, not the point. If the comment doesn't add value without the link, don't post it.
