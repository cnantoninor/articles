# Project Retrospective

*A living document updated after each milestone. Lessons feed forward into future planning.*

## Milestone: v2.0 — Article Completion

**Shipped:** 2026-03-14
**Phases:** 4 (9-12) | **Plans:** 4

### What Was Built
- Section II: Epistemic Debt definition with Ed integral formula, 6-dimension comparison table, 3 dramatic examples, industry statistics
- Article 0 (series overview) published on Substack
- Article 2 (Section II) published on Substack
- Established the 7-article series structure and reading paths for different audiences

### What Worked
- Mathematical definition + practitioner translation pattern (formula then "In simpler terms...")
- Sparse stats in text, detailed footnotes with URLs — maintains narrative flow
- Example sequencing by escalating stakes (financial → cost → safety)
- Shipping presentation (v1.0) first validated the Triangle framework before expanding to articles

### What Was Inefficient
- v2.0 scope was too ambitious (6 phases for Sections II-VII) — only Phase 12 was completed
- Phases 13-17 were roadmapped in detail but never started — wasted planning effort
- Gap between Phase 12 completion (2026-02-07) and milestone closure (2026-03-14) — 5 weeks idle
- Phase 01 orphaned on disk (not in roadmap) — cleanup debt

### Patterns Established
- Research-backed content: every quantified claim gets a footnoted citation with URL
- "You've felt this" voice: practitioner recognition before academic formalization
- Composite examples for sensitive domains (healthcare) with technical specificity
- Series format: individual articles that stand alone but build on each other

### Key Lessons
1. Scope milestones around what will actually ship, not aspirational full-article completion
2. For article series, one article per milestone phase is the right granularity
3. Published articles (Substack) are the real deliverable — GSD phases should map to publication events
4. The gap between "article drafted" and "article published" involves editing, formatting, and promotion that wasn't tracked

### Cost Observations
- Model mix: primarily sonnet for execution, opus for planning
- Notable: Phase 12 executed in ~2 minutes — content writing is fast when research is done

## Milestone: v1.0 — IRIS-2 Learnings Presentation

**Shipped:** 2026-02-07
**Phases:** 3 (9-11) | **Plans:** 3

### What Was Built
- 9-slide Marp presentation with Trade-off Triangle framework
- Concrete IRIS-2 examples for each vertex (DDD, E2E, workflow, meta)
- 4 actionable takeaways tied to specific file paths

### What Worked
- Same-day delivery (~20 minutes start to ship)
- File-path specificity made practices concrete and verifiable
- Vertex-by-vertex structure mapped cleanly to phases

### What Was Inefficient
- Nothing significant — small scope, fast execution

### Patterns Established
- Trade-off Triangle as central visual framework
- Meta practices as amplifiers, not vertices
- Non-prescriptive "conscious positioning" messaging

### Key Lessons
1. Ship small deliverables first to validate frameworks before expanding
2. Concrete file paths > abstract principles for practitioner audiences
3. Marp presentations are fast to produce with GSD workflow
