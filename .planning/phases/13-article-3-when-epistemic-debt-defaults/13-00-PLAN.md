*Overstated claims (2):*

1. **"Exponential" accumulation** — Article 1 (line 94) says "The velocity is exponential rather than linear" and Article 2's comparison table (line 158) says "Exponential with AI (entire modules in hours)." This is a MODEL PREDICTION from Ngabang's formula, not a measured empirical result. No study has measured exponential vs linear accumulation rates. The fact-check must flag this as ⚠️ OVERSTATED and recommend hedging language (e.g., "the model suggests," "potentially exponential," or "faster-than-linear"). Article 3 inherits this framing — if the pre-LLM vs post-LLM opening or any other section repeats the "exponential" claim, it must be hedged.

2. **Boehm "validated across decades"** — Article 2 (lines 39, 67, 95, 109) presents the Cost of Change Curve as settled science. In reality: (a) Boehm's original data is contested — later studies (e.g., agile literature) argue the curve is flatter in iterative development; (b) the L1-L4 layer mapping to Boehm's curve is the AUTHOR'S ADAPTATION, not Boehm's framework. The fact-check must flag this as ⚠️ OVERSTATED and recommend: explicitly acknowledge the adaptation ("Building on Boehm's framework, which some researchers contest in agile contexts, I propose...") and avoid implying Boehm himself validated the layer model. If Article 3 references Boehm's curve (e.g., in formula sidenotes or the pattern section), apply the same hedging.
