<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Opening hook
- Contrast scenario: code review in 2020 vs 2025
- Show both perspectives: the reviewer trying to understand, the author who may not fully understand their own submission
- AI involvement visibility: Claude to propose three options (explicit, implied, ambiguous reveal) for user to select during drafting

### Definitional approach
- Lead with "epistemic debt" — more digestible term than "epistemic warrant"
- Use example-first approach: show scenario where understanding is present vs absent, then name the concept
- Quick tech debt analogy: one sentence parallel ("like technical debt, but for understanding")
- Full tech debt comparison table comes in Phase 2
- Footnote approach for philosophy terminology: plain language in text, "epistemic warrant" in footnote for interested readers

### The shift narrative
- Frame intertwines role shift (construction → curation) with epistemic shift (knowing → not-knowing)
- Introduce the trade-off: complete ownership (no AI) vs velocity gained (AI production with partial ownership)
- Sweet spot positioned as the central question to explore — no answer given in opening
- Brief nod to polarized debate (AI-boosters vs skeptics), then move past it
- Time markers: Claude's discretion on whether to use exact years (2020/2025) or era framing

### Tone calibration
- Balanced: clear thesis framed as "here's what I've found worth examining"
- Stakes visible: make clear that getting this wrong has real consequences, without being alarmist
- Mixed voice: third person for observations, first person for thesis and framing
- Uncertainty handling: Claude's discretion on whether to acknowledge explicitly or let structure show it

### Claude's Discretion
- AI visibility approach selection (propose three options, user picks)
- Time marker specificity (exact years vs era framing)
- Uncertainty acknowledgment style
- Exact scenario details (characters, codebase, specific code being reviewed)

</decisions>
